Climate Justice Challenges

Jordan, like many other countries, faces various climate justice issues. These issues encompass both the responsibility of the nation to address climate change and the impact of climate change on vulnerable communities within the country.

Here are some climate justice issues specific to Jordan:

  • Water Scarcity:

    • Jordan is one of the most water-scarce countries in the world. Climate change has led to reduced rainfall and increased evaporation rates, exacerbating water scarcity issues. Jordan's efforts to adapt to and mitigate the impacts of water scarcity need to be equitable, ensuring that all segments of the population have access to clean and safe water.

  • Vulnerable Communities:

    • Jordan has a significant population of refugees, including those who have fled conflicts in neighboring countries. These refugees often live in vulnerable conditions and may be disproportionately affected by the impacts of climate change, including extreme heat, water scarcity, and displacement. Climate justice in Jordan includes addressing the unique vulnerabilities of these populations.

  • Energy Transition:

    • Jordan is actively pursuing a transition to renewable energy sources to reduce its carbon emissions. However, the social and economic impacts of this transition, especially on workers in traditional energy sectors, need to be managed equitably. Ensuring a just transition for these workers is an important climate justice issue.

  • Agriculture and Food Security:

    • Climate change can disrupt agricultural systems and food production. Jordan's efforts to adapt to these changes should consider the impact on small-scale farmers and marginalized communities who may rely on subsistence farming. Climate justice involves ensuring that these communities have the support and resources needed to adapt to changing conditions.

  • Health Impacts:

    • The health of Jordanian citizens, particularly vulnerable groups, can be affected by the increasing frequency and intensity of heatwaves and other climate-related health risks. Climate justice requires measures to protect the health of those most at risk, including the elderly, children, and low-income communities.

  • Migration and Displacement:

    • Climate change can exacerbate factors leading to internal and cross-border migration. Jordan hosts a significant refugee population, and climate-induced migration may further strain resources and increase the vulnerability of displaced people. Climate justice in this context involves planning for and assisting with the impacts of climate-induced migration.


    In summary, climate justice in Jordan involves a combination of mitigating the nation's contributions to climate change, such as reducing carbon emissions, and addressing the impacts of climate change on vulnerable communities, including refugees and marginalized groups. It's a multifaceted approach that aims to ensure that climate policies and adaptation strategies are equitable and protect those most at risk.
